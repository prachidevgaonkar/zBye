## What is zBye?

zBye is an agentic GPT that teaches Gen Z slang through gamification, memes, and adaptive scoring — not flashcards. Drop a word you don't understand, or let zBye quiz you. It explains, it scores, it clowns you (respectfully) when you miss. Then it levels you up.

Built for anyone who's ever read a comment section and felt absolutely lost.

---

## How it works

```
You drop a word or ask for a quiz
        ↓
zBye explains the slang with context + meme energy
        ↓
Quiz mode kicks in — use it right or get called out
        ↓
Score tracked, level updated, next word unlocked
```

zBye uses **adaptive agentic loops** — it tracks your performance across the conversation, adjusts difficulty, and keeps score without you lifting a finger. It's not just answering — it's teaching, testing, and reacting.

## Features

- 🧠 **Slang explainer** — definitions with real context, not dictionary-dry
- 🎮 **Quiz mode** — use the word in a sentence, zBye judges the vibe
- 📊 **Adaptive scoring** — gets harder as you get better
- 😂 **Meme-based engagement** — reactions tuned to Gen Z energy
- 🔁 **Agentic memory** — tracks your session score and progress mid-convo
- 🎯 **Level system** — Rookie → Sheesh → No Cap → Slay certified

## Why I built this

I wanted to explore what gamification looks like inside a conversational AI — no UI, no buttons, no points bar on screen. Just an agent that tracks state, adapts, and rewards through language alone.

zBye is my testbed for **agentic gamification patterns** — scoring logic in prompts, adaptive difficulty through YAML-structured instructions, and engagement mechanics that work inside a chat window. The same principles apply when I design gamified adoption frameworks for enterprise programs at scale.

## The agent design challenge

Most GPTs answer and stop. zBye had to **track, adapt, and react** — all through prompt design alone, no code, no database. The interesting problems:

- How do you maintain score state across a conversation without persistent memory?
- How do you make difficulty feel earned, not random?
- How do you make an AI feel playful without losing coherence?

These are the same questions that come up in agentic enterprise systems — just with higher stakes and fewer memes.
